<p><br />The left column usually provides the dates of birth and death.<br />The characters to denote birth on headstones<br />生於 or 生于<br />Sarng Yu &ndash; born in<br />(Yu &ndash; the second character in traditional and simplified forms)<br />The characters to denote death on headstones<br />終於 or 終于<br />Zhong Yu &ndash; in the end / finally<br />(Yu &ndash; the second character in traditional and simplified forms)<br />The Chinese write locations and dates from &ldquo;large to small&rdquo;, that is, country, city/town, village; year, month, day.<br />年 &ndash; Neen &ndash; year<br />月 &ndash; Yuet &ndash; month<br />曰 &ndash; Yut &ndash; day<br />It can be complicated as dates can be referred to by Dynasty or from the Republic, as well as by the Chinese lunar calendar or western Gregorian calendar.<br />Numbers are usually written in Chinese numerals (1 to 10)<br />Normal 一 二 三 四 五 六 七 八 九 十<br />Financial 壹 貳 叁 肆 伍 陸 柒 捌 玖 拾<br />Financial numbers are used by businesses such as banks. The characters are difficult to alter/forge, unlike normal numbers.<br />There is also another set of numbers used by merchants.</p>
